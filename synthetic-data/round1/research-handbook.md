# IDRI Research Handbook
*Version 3.1 — Effective January 2026*

Welcome to the International Dodo Research Institute. This handbook covers access protocols, data workflows, and how to work with the Cockpit knowledge system.

---

## The Cockpit

The **Cockpit** is IDRI's institutional AI research assistant. It holds:
- Specimen catalog and morphological consensus
- Field notes from active excavations (Mare aux Songes)
- Inter-institutional loan status
- Published literature summaries (post-2015)
- Open research questions and their current standing

**Access:** Via Rajan Pillai's team. New researchers request a Cockpit account by emailing rajan.pillai@idri-mauritius.org with their institutional affiliation and research area.

**Cockpit is read-only for field researchers.** Write access (to update consensus entries or add specimens) requires a co-sign from the relevant lead (Kofi for osteology, Isabelle for diet/isotopes, Miriam for specimen records).

---

## Specimen Access

### Digital access (CT scans, 3D models)
Available to all IDRI researchers via the Cockpit. No approval required. Cite as: *IDRI Digital Archive, accessed [date].*

### Physical specimen access (at partner institutions)
1. Submit a loan request form to Miriam van der Berg (miriam.vdberg@naturalis.nl).
2. Miriam coordinates with the holding institution. Lead time: **4–8 weeks** for inter-institutional loans.
3. For urgent access (e.g., active publication deadline), Eleanor Marsh can fast-track requests — contact her directly.
4. Handling of original specimens requires proof of insurance and institution-level MOU.

## Field Site: Mare aux Songes

Mare aux Songes (southeast Mauritius) is IDRI's primary active excavation. Thomas Krüger leads the site.

- **Active excavation season:** May–October (dry season)
- **Off-season:** Site sealed and monitored; no unsupervised access
- **To join an excavation:** Contact Thomas Krüger (t.krueger@idri-mauritius.org) at least 3 months before the season opens
- **Field safety:** All personnel require tropical fieldwork certification and a pre-season medical clearance

---

## Naming & versioning conventions

- Specimens: use the institution prefix + collection number (e.g., RMNH.AVE.40, NHM-OXF-001)
- Consensus documents: versioned as `vX.Y` — major version for paradigm changes, minor for corrections
- CT scans: archived under `specimens/{ID}/ct/` in the Cockpit; resolution noted in filename
