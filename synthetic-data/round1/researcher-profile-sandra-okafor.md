## Background

Sandra grew up in Lagos, Nigeria, where her father was a secondary school biology teacher. She moved to the UK at 17 to study Natural Sciences at Cambridge (BA, 2004; PhD, 2009 — thesis: *Morphological convergence in flightless island birds of the Indian Ocean*). She joined the NHM as a postdoc in 2010 and has been a senior curator since 2017.

She is one of roughly a dozen people in the world who have handled the Oxford Dodo specimen directly, and she has done so more times than anyone alive. Her relationship with the specimen is, by her own description, "professionally inappropriate."

---

## Research focus

Sandra's work sits at the intersection of **museum curation, avian morphology, and extinction science**. Her main contributions to dodo research:

- Led the 2019 re-CT of NHM-OXF-001 (the Oxford Dodo skull and foot), producing the highest-resolution scans currently in existence. All scans archived in the IDRI Cockpit under `specimens/NHM-OXF-001/`.
- Co-authored the 2021 paper on soft tissue reconstruction from the dried head — the paper that challenged the traditional beak curvature measurements and revised the dietary range estimate.
- Currently leading a collaboration with the University of Copenhagen on ancient DNA extraction from the Oxford specimen's bone marrow. Results expected late 2026.

**Selected publications:**
- Okafor, S. & Marsh, E. (2021). *Revised beak morphometrics of Raphus cucullatus from CT re-analysis of NHM-OXF-001.* Zoological Journal of the Linnean Society, 193(4), 1102–1119.
- Okafor, S. (2018). *Island gigantism and flightlessness in Mascarene avifauna: a comparative study.* Ibis, 160(2), 378–394.
- Okafor, S., van der Berg, M., & Asamoah, K. (2023). *Cross-institutional imaging protocols for subfossil Columbiformes.* Journal of Natural History, 57(1–4), 45–61.

## Working style & personality

Sandra is known for being direct and meticulous. She keeps exceptionally detailed lab notes and is the person who will politely but firmly correct a misattributed specimen in a paper's supplementary materials. She prefers email over Slack, and responds quickly during London working hours (GMT/BST).

She is strongly protective of the Oxford Dodo material and will not approve loan requests for physical elements unless there is a compelling scientific case and full institutional MOU in place. CT scan access is freely given — she considers open data sharing non-negotiable.

She gives a public lecture at the NHM once a year ("What killed the dodo — and why it wasn't just sailors") which consistently sells out.

## How to work with Sandra

- **For CT scan access:** Contact Rajan Pillai (IDRI Cockpit) — scans already archived, no need to involve Sandra.
- **For physical specimen access (NHM-OXF-001):** Contact Miriam van der Berg first. Miriam coordinates with Sandra. Do not contact Sandra directly for loan requests — this bypasses the institutional process and she will redirect you anyway.
- **For research collaboration:** Email directly. She is collegial and open to joint work, especially anything involving the ancient DNA project or the soft tissue material.
- **For media / press:** NHM has a press office. Sandra does not respond to press requests sent to her personal email.
