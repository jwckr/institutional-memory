# IDRI Specimen Catalog & Morphological Consensus
*Version 2.4 — Effective January 2026*
*Owned by Dr. Kofi Asamoah & Miriam van der Berg*

This document is the institute's authoritative record of known *Raphus cucullatus* specimens and the current scientific consensus on morphology. Entries are updated after each publication cycle.

---

## Known Major Specimens

### RMNH.AVE.40 — Leiden Complete Skeleton
- **Location:** Naturalis Biodiversity Centre, Leiden
- **Completeness:** ~85% (most complete articulated skeleton in existence)
- **Provenance:** Mare aux Songes subfossil deposit, excavated 1863
- **Status:** Reference specimen for all morphometric work
- **CT scan available:** Yes — archived in Cockpit under `specimens/RMNH-AVE-40/`

### Oxford Dodo (NHM-OXF-001)
- **Location:** Natural History Museum London (on loan from Oxford University Museum)
- **Completeness:** Skull + soft tissue head (dried), right foot preserved
- **Provenance:** Possibly a live specimen brought to Europe, pre-1650
- **Status:** Only specimen with preserved soft tissue; used for plumage and integument research
- **CT scan available:** Yes — archived under `specimens/NHM-OXF-001/`

### MNHN-PAL-0032 & MNHN-PAL-0033 — Paris Partial Skeletons
- **Location:** Musée National d'Histoire Naturelle, Paris
- **Completeness:** ~40% each; complementary elements
- **Provenance:** Mare aux Songes, excavated 1904
- **CT scan available:** Partial — femur and pelvis only

### Prague Tarsometatarsus (NMP-AV-1124)
- **Location:** National Museum Prague
- **Completeness:** Single element, excellent preservation
- **Notes:** Key element for locomotion modelling. Kofi's 2023 biomechanical paper is based on this specimen.

## Morphological Consensus (as of Jan 2026)

### Body mass
**Consensus estimate: 21.1 kg (± 1.8 kg)**
Source: Kitchener (1993) revised by Angst et al. (2011). Based on hindlimb bone robusticity scaling against extant Columbiformes and ratites.

### Height (standing)
**~95–105 cm** to top of head. Source: RMNH.AVE.40 articulation study (van der Berg & Marsh, 2022).

### Plumage colouration
**Consensus: uniform grey-brown dorsally, lighter grey ventrally; yellow-white tail tuft.**
Primary sources: Dutch VOC illustrations (1600–1662), specifically the Roelant Savery paintings (Savery 1626 considered most reliable). Yellow hooked beak, pale yellow legs.

### Diet
**Primarily frugivorous + seed predator.** Evidence: gastroliths (rounded quartz pebbles, 15–30 mm) found in situ at Mare aux Songes; molar-shaped palate for crushing. Secondary hypothesis (shellfish/crabs) is speculative and not supported by isotope data.
Source: Fontaine et al. (2023) — carbon/nitrogen isotope ratios from RMNH.AVE.40 ribs.

### Locomotion
**Slow, terrestrial, non-cursorial.** Femur and tarsometatarsus angles indicate a wide-stance walk. Not capable of sustained running. Kofi's 2023 Prague paper sets maximum walking speed at ~1.8 m/s.

### Extinction date
**Last confirmed sighting: 1681 (Volkert Evertsz record).**
Extinction conventionally dated to ~1693 based on absence from records. Earlier date of 1662 proposed by some; IDRI consensus follows 1681.

## Open Research Questions

1. Sexual dimorphism — no confirmed male/female skeleton pair yet identified
2. Nesting behaviour — no nest material or egg clutch found in situ
3. Juvenile growth rate — only one putative juvenile specimen (fragmentary, Mare aux Songes)
4. Population size at European contact — estimates range from 4,000 to 45,000 individuals
