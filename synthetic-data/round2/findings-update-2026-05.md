# IDRI Research Update — May 2026
*Effective 2026-05-12. Supersedes relevant sections of Specimen Catalog v2.4.*
*Authored by Dr. Kofi Asamoah & Dr. Sven Larsen. Reviewed by Prof. Dr. Eleanor Marsh.*

This update documents two significant findings from the spring 2026 analysis cycle. Both contradict previously held consensus positions and require the Cockpit to update its morphological knowledge.

---

## Finding 1 — Body mass revision (CRITICAL UPDATE)

**Previous consensus:** 21.1 kg (± 1.8 kg) — Kitchener 1993 / Angst 2011

**New consensus: 12.4 kg (± 1.1 kg)**

### What changed
Kofi Asamoah and Sven Larsen applied cortical bone cross-sectional geometry analysis (CSGA) to RMNH.AVE.40 and the Prague tarsometatarsus. This method, standard in mammal palaeontology but only recently applied to Columbiformes, yields body mass estimates from bone wall thickness rather than total robusticity.

The Kitchener/Angst estimates extrapolated from ratite scaling — but dodos are not ratites. They are extreme island-adapted pigeons. Their bones are structurally more similar to large flightless rails than to ostriches. The old scaling law overcorrected by ~70%.

**Published:** Asamoah, K. & Larsen, S. (2026). *Body mass estimation in Raphus cucullatus via cortical bone geometry: a revision.* Journal of Vertebrate Paleontology, 46(2).

### Implications
- All biomechanical models using the 21 kg figure must be recalculated (Kofi's Prague locomotion paper is under revision)
- Population carrying-capacity estimates based on old mass are also affected
- The "obese dodo" trope in popular depictions is likely an artefact of captive birds brought to Europe, not wild body condition

## Finding 2 — Plumage colouration revision

**Previous consensus:** Uniform grey-brown dorsally, lighter grey ventrally; yellow-white tail tuft

**New finding:** Evidence suggests a **more pronounced brown-rufous dorsal colouration** with a **distinctive pale cream breast patch**, inconsistent with the uniform grey interpretation.

### What changed
Dr. Isabelle Fontaine obtained high-resolution multispectral imaging of a previously unstudied VOC-era gouache held in a private collection (Netherlands, owner anonymous, now on loan to MNHN Paris). The painting, dated ca. 1638 based on paper and pigment analysis, shows a bird with strong rufous-brown dorsal plumage and a clearly demarcated cream-white breast — not the uniform grey of the Savery paintings.

Cross-referencing with the written account of VOC officer Pieter van den Broecke (1617): *"brownish-grey above, lighter below, with a great white tuft"* — previously interpreted loosely, now better matched by the new image.

**Working paper:** Fontaine, I. (2026, preprint). *Multispectral re-analysis of a 1638 dodo gouache: implications for plumage reconstruction.* bioRxiv. Pending peer review — treat as preliminary but credible.

### Status
This finding is **not yet formal consensus**. The Specimen Catalog will be updated to v2.5 once peer review is complete. In the meantime, when asked about plumage, the Cockpit should note that the grey consensus is currently under challenge and cite both positions.
